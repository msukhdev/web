Ferrofluid

Ferrofluids are synthetical suspensions of solid nano-sized magnetic particles stable dispersed in a liquid carrier medium. Since these magnetic particles are nanosized (~ 10nm diameter) hence each particle behaves like a single magnetic domain. The speciality of the ferrofluid is that it exhibits fluid and magnetic properties simultaneously. The magnetic nanoparticles are mixed in a carrier fluid in such a way that it flows with the molecules of the carrier fluid and the whole combination behaves like a normal fluid with magnetic properties. In the application of a magnetic field, the whole combination of fluids flows towards the direction of the applied field. Ferrofluids are easier to handle because the fluid location can be controlled through the use of a magnetic field. They can be forced to flow by varying the strength of the magnetic field. These properties of ferrofluids open a wide range of exciting and promising applications.
